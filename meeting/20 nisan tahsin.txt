Burak Akc's Zoom Meeting - April 20
VIEW RECORDING: 
Meeting Purpose

To explore a potential partnership between Tahsin's and Cortex's diaspora community projects.

Key Takeaways

  - Cortex's Proposal: Cortex offered Tahsin a partnership to become a City Partner (e.g., for Sydney), integrating his community into its platform. This provides a structured career path with revenue share (6–10% of user spend) and event-based income, solving the monetization challenge for influencers.
  - Tahsin's Counter-Proposal: Tahsin plans to launch his own platform, focusing on teaching social media marketing to diaspora professionals (doctors, businesses) to help them grow their practices.
  - Decision Deferred: Tahsin will evaluate the Cortex offer after his platform's launch webinar in ~2 weeks. Both parties agreed to maintain communication and support each other's efforts.

Topics

The Problem: Disorganized Diaspora

  - Both projects address the same core issues:
      - Lack of Trust: Newcomers are often exploited by established members.
      - Isolation: Individuals feel alone and lack a reliable support network.
      - Inefficient Networking: Information is scattered across fragmented WhatsApp groups, making it hard to find reliable services (doctors, tradespeople) or connect professionally.
      - Missed Opportunities: The diaspora's collective talent is underutilized due to a lack of organization, while other groups (e.g., Indian diaspora) effectively leverage their networks for economic and political power.

Tahsin's Project: A Social Media Academy

  - Background: Tahsin, a physicist turned social media marketer, grew his partner's account to 100k+ followers and launched two successful Pilates studios in Japan.
  - Vision: Launch a platform to teach social media marketing to diaspora professionals.
      - Goal: Empower members to grow their businesses and professional practices.
      - Model: A paid subscription service with tiered content (e.g., free Instagram basics, paid YouTube masterclasses).
      - Status: A custom app is in performance testing, with a launch webinar scheduled for ~2 weeks.

Cortex's Project: A Global Diaspora Ecosystem

  - Background: Cortex is a Qualtron Sinclair-backed project led by Burak Akcakanat (strategy) and Umut Barış Terzioğlu (community).
  - Vision: A global platform to organize the diaspora, connecting individuals, businesses, and associations.
      - Features: A comprehensive directory, AI-powered matching, and a "relocation engine" to guide newcomers.
      - Business Model: Paid subscriptions from end-users and businesses.
      - Strategic Advantage: Cortex is positioned in the GCC, a region with government and investor interest in diaspora solutions, providing access to significant funding.

The Proposed Partnership: Cortex City Partner

  - Offer: Tahsin becomes the City Partner for a region (e.g., Sydney), integrating his community into the Cortex platform.
  - Career Path: A structured journey from Ambassador → City/Country Partner → Agency Owner.
  - Revenue Model:
      - Revenue Share: 6–10% of all spend by users Tahsin refers (e.g., subscriptions, event tickets).
      - Event-Based Income: Partners build businesses by organizing large-scale events (e.g., New Year's parties, tours) using Cortex's platform for ticketing and promotion.
  - Rationale: This provides a stable, scalable business model for influencers, solving the monetization and infrastructure challenges they face working alone.
