Topics

Problem: Diaspora Fragmentation & Inefficiency

  - The Turkish diaspora is fragmented across countless WhatsApp groups, creating significant inefficiencies.
  - Primary Pain Points:
      - Repeated Questions: The same basic questions (e.g., "Which doctor?") are asked endlessly.
      - Ineffective Networking: Physical events yield low-quality connections, and the right people are hard to find.
      - Lack of Trusted Experts: Finding verified professionals (lawyers, consultants) is difficult, exposing individuals to scams.
          - Example: A US-based scammer defrauded ~100 people of $150k each by promising a 12% annual ROI on property, then vanished after 3 months of payouts.
  - Root Cause: A "survival society" mindset prioritizes short-term gains over long-term cooperation, hindering effective organization.

Solution: "Cortex" Global Platform

  - The proposed solution is a global platform, tentatively named "Cortex," to centralize diaspora resources.
  - Core Features:
      - Verified Expert Marketplace: A directory of vetted professionals with profiles, CTAs, and contact info.
      - AI-Powered Tools:
          - AI Twins: Digital versions of experts for paid, on-demand consultations (e.g., a 20-min session for €50).
          - Content Generation: AI to create social media posts for experts, boosting their visibility.
      - Centralized Event Management: A hub for all diaspora events, handling ticketing and registrations.
      - Community Integration: Bots to pull information from existing WhatsApp/Telegram groups into the platform.

Strategic Alignment & Collaboration

  - The project aligns with both participants' existing work:
      - Barış's "101" Initiative: A local German version of the platform, currently a WhatsApp Community for Cağaloğlu alumni.
      - Burak's "Cortex" Vision: A global platform, conceptually demonstrated by the "Consultants" section on his Payal real estate site.
  - Partnership Model: A global-local structure where Barış leads the German market implementation.
  - Timeline: A long-term project ("marathon") with a 6–8 month MVP timeline.
